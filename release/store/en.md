## Long Description

Little Mandarin Classics is a daily Mandarin reading app for overseas Chinese and bilingual families with children ages 5-8.

Each story is designed for a short 5-8 minute reading habit. Children read child-safe adaptations of Chinese classics, starting with a Three Kingdoms series based on public-domain source material and rewritten for young readers. The stories focus on wisdom, cooperation, courage, kindness, and cultural connection.

This is not a full Chinese course, a replacement for Chinese school, or an open AI chat app. It is a focused reading companion that helps children practice reading one complete story at a time.

### What Children Can Do

- Read short Chinese classic stories written for early readers
- Turn pinyin on or off while reading
- Listen with audio / TTS support
- Review 5-8 story words with pinyin and English definitions
- Answer 3 gentle comprehension questions
- Use a retell prompt to talk about the story
- Ask for controlled AI explanations about the current story only

### What Parents Can See

- Stories read
- Reading days
- Quiz progress
- Words reviewed
- Story-by-story progress

Parent progress is designed for home support, not public comparison. Little Mandarin Classics does not ask for a child's real name or personal details. Parent contact or feedback, if offered, is optional and parent-only. Analytics are anonymous and do not include child personal information.

### First Story Series

The first series adapts selected Three Kingdoms stories for children, including familiar moments such as the Oath of the Peach Garden, Borrowing Arrows with Boats, and the Empty Fort Strategy. Battle details are softened for age-appropriate reading, with emphasis on choices, friendship, responsibility, wisdom, and renyi.

### Built For Bilingual Families

Little Mandarin Classics supports families where children may understand some spoken Chinese but need help reading Chinese text independently. English definitions, bilingual titles, pinyin, audio, and short story sessions lower the reading barrier while keeping the story text in Chinese.
