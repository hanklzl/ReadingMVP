# Seed User Screening Form Draft

Purpose: screen parent/guardian volunteers for the Little Mandarin Classics MVP seed test. This form is for adults only. Do not ask a child to complete it.

Product summary to show at the top of the form:

> Little Mandarin Classics is a short daily Mandarin reading app for overseas Chinese and bilingual families with children ages 5-8. The MVP includes child-safe Three Kingdoms stories, pinyin/audio support, tap-word vocabulary help, 3 comprehension questions, parent progress, and controlled AI explanations about the current story only.

Privacy note to show before any questions:

> Please do not enter a child's real name, nickname, school name, teacher name, class name, birthday, photo, voice recording, exact address, contact details, or other child personal information. We only ask for parent/guardian contact information and broad, parent-reported learning context.

Compliance note to show near the privacy note:

> This MVP follows COPPA/GDPR-K data-minimization principles. Child progress is designed to remain local or anonymous. If a local read-aloud/retell recording feature is used, child voice is not uploaded; please do not send recordings in feedback.

## Internal Use Rules

- Use parent contact as the only direct identifier.
- Store child context only as broad ranges or tags: age band, reading level band, language exposure, device, broad region/timezone, and feedback willingness.
- Do not store school names, teacher names, class names, child birthdays, exact addresses, child photos, child voice recordings, child contact details, health/diagnosis information, or raw child messages.
- Keep child progress local or anonymous; do not create child account identifiers from screening responses.
- Do not request or upload child voice. If a parent sends a recording by mistake, do not retain or copy it into tracking notes.
- If a parent accidentally provides child PII, do not copy it into tracking notes. Redact it before internal review.
- Do not invite families if the form was completed by a child or asks the team to contact a child directly.
