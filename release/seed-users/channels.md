# Seed User Recruitment Channels Draft

Status: draft planning artifact only. No outreach has been completed by this file.

Audience: overseas Chinese and bilingual parents/guardians of children ages 5-8. Do not target or message children.

## Priority Family Contexts

- Overseas Chinese or bilingual families with children ages 5-8.
- Families where children understand some Chinese but need help reading Chinese text independently.
- Families already trying weekend Chinese school, heritage-language school, online class, Chinese picture books/readers, learning apps, or parent-led home reading.
- Families looking for a short reading supplement, not a full curriculum, live tutoring, exam prep, or open AI chat.

## Broad Region Priorities

Use broad region targeting for outreach planning, not exact child location collection.

- United States: overseas Chinese parent groups, Mandarin immersion communities, Chinese school parent groups, bilingual-family communities.
- Canada: Chinese parent groups, Chinese community centers, heritage-language school communities.
- Australia: Mandarin/Cantonese family groups, weekend Chinese class communities, local Chinese associations.
- Singapore: English-dominant Chinese families looking for low-pressure reading practice and parent-child retell prompts.
- Other English-dominant overseas communities: use the same parent-only, opt-in screening rules.

## Outreach Do's

- Do contact parents, guardians, adult group admins, teachers, coordinators, and community organizers.
- Do ask permission before posting in private groups.
- Do use an opt-in screening link completed by parents/guardians only.
- Do describe the product as a 5-8 minute daily Mandarin reading supplement.
- Do state that controlled AI explains the current story only and is not open chat.
- Do state the privacy baseline when space allows: COPPA/GDPR-K data minimization, parent-only contact, local or anonymous child progress, and no child voice uploads.
- Do emphasize child-safe Three Kingdoms adaptations focused on wisdom, cooperation, courage, kindness, and cultural connection.
- Do track channel quality in aggregate: submissions, qualified parent signups, device fit, first-week story completion, parent feedback response.
- Do keep follow-up messages parent-written and parent-facing.

## Outreach Don'ts

- Do not message, target, or solicit children directly.
- Do not ask a child to fill out a form, comment, DM, submit audio, or share a reading video.
- Do not collect child real names, nicknames, school names, teacher names, class names, photos, videos, birthdays, exact addresses, child emails, child phone numbers, child social handles, or voice recordings.
- Do not ask parents to upload child voice or retell recordings.
- Do not ask schools, teachers, or parent volunteers for rosters, student lists, class lists, or family contact exports.
- Do not use identifiable child photos, classroom images, school logos, uniforms, certificates, or screenshots with child data.
- Do not market battle scenes, violence, fear, or dramatic conflict from Three Kingdoms.
- Do not promise fluency, grade improvement, test-score gains, therapeutic benefits, or guaranteed reading outcomes.
- Do not present draft outreach as already posted, approved, endorsed, or completed.

## Channel Notes

### Parent Groups

Best for early discovery because parents can self-select. Use bilingual copy when group language is mixed. Keep comments and DMs parent-only; if someone shares child details, reply with a reminder to avoid child personal information.

### Weekend Chinese Schools And Heritage-Language Schools

Best for families already seeking Chinese support. The safest ask is a coordinator-forwarded parent blurb with the screening link. Do not ask for student names, class lists, teacher notes about individual children, or school identifiers in the form.

### Community Organizations

Best for credibility and broad local reach. Contact adult organizers at cultural centers, family associations, libraries, and parent meetups. Materials should be informational and opt-in, not a sign-up sheet at a child event.

### Social Platforms

Best for message testing and reaching families outside existing networks. Use parent-facing captions, avoid identifiable child media, disclose that this is an MVP recruitment post, and follow each group's posting rules.

### Referrals

Best after initial parents understand the product. Ask parents to forward the public screening link. Do not accept a spreadsheet, phone list, WeChat contact list, or direct introduction containing another family's private contact without that parent's opt-in.
