# Seed User Recruitment Plan

Goal: recruit and validate 100 overseas Chinese/bilingual families with children age 5-8. Parent contact only. Priority markets are the United States, Canada, Australia, and Singapore.

Core positioning:

- Daily 5-8 minute Chinese classic story reading for overseas Chinese/bilingual children age 5-8.
- Pinyin, audio, vocabulary with English definitions, 3 comprehension questions, and parent progress report.
- Child-safe controlled AI explanation that only supports the current story, not open chat.
- First MVP series: child-safe Three Kingdoms stories adapted for age 5-8.
- Not a replacement for Chinese school or live tutoring; it is a daily reading supplement.

MVP validation signals:

- 100 parent-consented seed families.
- In week 1, each invited family is encouraged to complete 2+ stories.
- Story completion rate: 40% baseline, 70% excellent.
- Parent report open rate shows whether parents care about visible progress.
- At least 10 of 100 families explicitly say they would pay if the product works well.
- D7 retention around 30% is the signal to expand recruitment beyond the seed cohort.

## Priority Communities

United States:

- Bay Area, Los Angeles, New York/New Jersey, Seattle, Boston.
- Parent groups around Chinese schools, Mandarin immersion, bilingual families, and heritage language learning.

Canada:

- Toronto and Vancouver.
- Local Chinese school communities, community centers, and Chinese parent groups.

Australia:

- Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane.
- Mandarin/Cantonese family groups, weekend Chinese class communities, and parent social groups.

Singapore:

- English-dominant Chinese families looking for low-pressure Chinese reading practice.
- Position as reading interest, classic culture, and parent-child retell practice, not tuition or exam prep.

## 4-Week Outreach And Follow-Up Plan

### Week 1: Launch And First 40 Families

Actions:

- Publish WeChat posts in priority city/community groups after admin approval.
- Contact 10-20 Chinese school teachers or administrators with the opt-in forward blurb.
- Post once on Xiaohongshu and once on Instagram/TikTok with the parent privacy note.
- Start a tracking sheet with channel, market, city/metro, child age range, language level, device, feedback willingness, and pay-willingness signal.

Targets:

- 60-80 screening submissions.
- 40 qualified families selected or waitlisted.
- At least 60% from priority markets.

Follow-up message at 48 hours:

> Thanks for your interest in Little Mandarin Classics. We are reviewing parent screening forms and inviting families in small waves by age, market, Chinese level, and device fit. If invited, week 1 will be simple: try 2+ short stories and answer a brief parent feedback form.

### Week 2: Fill Market And Level Gaps

Actions:

- Identify underrepresented markets or levels, for example Singapore, Canada, Australia, age 5, or early readers.
- Post targeted copy in Reddit/FB groups and city-specific parent groups.
- Ask accepted parents for referrals using the opt-in referral script.
- Send a short reminder to incomplete form submissions if the parent contact is valid.

Targets:

- 120-150 total submissions.
- 80 qualified families selected or waitlisted.
- Balanced spread across age 5, 6, 7, and 8 when possible.

Reminder:

> We are closing the first seed cohort soon. If your family can try 2+ stories in the first week and provide quick weekly parent feedback, please complete the screening form here: {screening_form_link}. Parent contact only; no child names or school names needed.

### Week 3: Onboard Wave 1 And Continue Recruiting Wave 2

Actions:

- Invite the first wave of families.
- Send a parent-only onboarding note with the two-story first-week goal.
- Continue recruiting only for missing segments, not broad volume.
- Track week 1 activation: invited, started, completed 1 story, completed 2+ stories, parent report opened.

Onboarding note:

> Welcome to the Little Mandarin Classics MVP. For the first week, please help your child try at least 2 stories. Each story should take about 5-8 minutes. Afterward, we will send a short parent feedback form. Please do not send child names, school names, photos, birthdays, exact addresses, or recordings in feedback.

Day 3 nudge:

> Quick check-in: if your family has not tried a story yet, this is a good time to try one 5-8 minute story. The most useful first-week signal is whether your child can complete 2+ stories and whether the parent report feels worth opening.

### Week 4: Close Cohort And Validate Expansion Signals

Actions:

- Close the first cohort at 100 invited families.
- Keep extra qualified families on a waitlist for the next wave.
- Send a week 1 feedback form to active families.
- Ask direct willingness-to-pay and price-sensitivity questions after parents have seen the product.
- Summarize channel quality: submissions, qualified rate, activation, 2+ story completion, report opens, weekly feedback response, and pay willingness.

Week 1 feedback ask:

> Thank you for testing. This week's feedback should take 3-5 minutes. We are looking at whether children complete 2+ stories, whether each story feels the right length/difficulty, whether the parent report is useful, and whether families would pay for this if it works well. Please keep feedback parent-written and avoid child names, school names, photos, birthdays, addresses, or other child personal information.

Expansion decision:

- Expand beyond the seed cohort if D7 retention is around 30% or higher, story completion is at least 40%, parent report opens show clear interest, feedback quality is high, and at least 10 of 100 families say they are willing to pay.
- Iterate before expanding if families join but do not complete 2+ stories, completion is far below 40%, parents do not open reports, or feedback says the stories are too hard, too long, or not motivating.

## Privacy-Safe Guardrails

Recruitment rules:

- Only parents or legal guardians may submit the screening form.
- Never ask children to fill out forms, message the team, or provide contact information.
- Never collect child real names, school names, teacher names, photos, videos, birthdays, exact addresses, child emails, child phone numbers, or child social handles.
- Do not ask teachers, schools, or class coordinators to share rosters or student contact lists.
- Use opt-in parent links only.
- City or metro area is enough; do not request street address, ZIP/postal code, or school district.
- Ask age range only: 5, 6, 7, 8, or outside 5-8. Do not ask for birthdate.
- Do not collect health, diagnosis, immigration, income, or sensitive family information.
- Public social posts must not use identifiable child images or classroom images.
- Feedback should be parent-written. If a parent sends child PII by mistake, do not copy it into tracking notes.

Product trust language:

- Say "controlled AI explanation" rather than "AI friend" or "chatbot."
- Say "only explains the current story" in every AI-related script.
- Say "child-safe adaptation" for Three Kingdoms content; do not market battles, violence, or dramatic conflict.
- Say "daily reading supplement" rather than "replace Chinese school" or "guaranteed improvement."
- Avoid claims about test scores, fluency guarantees, or medical/therapeutic outcomes.

Tracking rules:

- Use parent contact as the only direct identifier.
- Store child context as ranges or tags, for example age 6, early reader, Mandarin + English home, weekend class yes/no.
- Keep school/class status as categorical. Do not store names of schools, teachers, classes, or classmates.
- Track anonymous MVP metrics: invited, started, story completed, 2+ stories in week 1, parent report opened, feedback submitted, willingness to pay.
- Report cohort results in aggregate only, for example "Bay Area early readers" or "Singapore age 7-8 families," never individual child details.

## Channel Quality Review

Review every week:

- Qualified family rate by channel.
- Cost/time per qualified family.
- Market mix: US, Canada, Australia, Singapore.
- Age mix: 5, 6, 7, 8.
- Reading level mix: few characters, phrases, sentences with support, simple paragraphs.
- Activation: started first story.
- Week 1 signal: completed 2+ stories.
- Story completion rate against 40% baseline and 70% excellent line.
- Parent report open rate.
- Weekly feedback response rate.
- Willingness-to-pay count toward 10/100.
- D7 retention against the 30% expansion signal.

Prioritize the channels that produce families who actually complete stories and submit feedback, not just the channels that produce the most form submissions.
